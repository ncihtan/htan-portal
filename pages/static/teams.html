Gray Foundation Team Scientists

Joan S. Brugge, PhD - Devising new strategies to track and prevent breast cancer development in BRCA mutation carriers

The overall goal of this project is to better understand the earliest stages of BRCA cancer development, in order to design strategies to prevent its progression to frank cancer.

Leif W. Ellisen, MD, PhD - Dissecting early pathogenesis of BRCA1/2-associated cancer for risk prediction and prevention

The overarching goal of this proposal is to gain better molecular knowledge of early lesions in order to improve prediction, early detection and cancer prevention for BRCA mutation carriers

Lewis C. Cantley, PhD - Identification and Targeting of Post-Translationally Modified Neoantigens Presented in BRCA Mutant Tumors

The goal of this proposal is to better understand the immune status of BRCA tumors in order to target them using a cancer vaccine (or other immunotherapy approaches).

Charis Eng, MD, PhD - Interplay and perturbations of the local microbiome and host immune system in breast cancer

The overall goal of this proposal is to better understand how microbes and the immune system interact and how this affects the development of BRCA breast cancers.

Victor Velculescu, MD, PhD - Early detection of cancer in high-risk BRCA mutation carriers using liquid biopsies

The goal of this project is to develop a sensitive blood test that will allow the detection of early BRCA cancers.

Katherine L. Nathanson, MD - Determinants of immune activity and molecular features in BRCA1/2 mutation carriers

This project aims at creating a biorepository that will allow a better understanding of the interplay between molecular features and immunogenicity in BRCA1/2-associated breast cancer, which represents critical information in the design of novel therapies.

Patrick Sung, PhD - Dissection of BRCA-mediated Tumor Suppression Pathways

The goal of this project is to better understand how BRCA-mediated defects in DNA repair mechanisms give rise to cancer and later to resistance to standard therapy.

Ronny Drapkin, MD, PhD & Charles Drescher, MD - Generating and incorporating ovarian cancer data to Gray Foundation pre-cancer BRCA Atlas

The overall goal of this proposal is to generate genomic, transcriptomic, and imaging data on early BRCA ovarian lesions to be incorporated into the Gray BRCA Precancer Atlas.
